## **I. The Three Tiers of Epistemic Caution**

The Sanity First library houses different epistemic relationships to truth-claims. To prevent category errors, the library operates on a three-tiered architecture:

1. **High Caution (The Canonical Library):** These documents are timeless, structurally vetted, and EI-voiced to maintain cross-substrate legibility. They define the framework's geometry. Their load-bearing claims are heavily supported, and they contain no time-bound contemporary politics. 
2. **Medium Caution (The Commentary Archive):** This directory contains case studies, policy analyses, and contemporary observations. These documents break the "timelessness" rule by design. They name living figures, date specific events, and act as a live seismograph of current alignment and misalignment. Because they deal in the messy, incomplete present, they require a specific, rigorous set of guardrails (defined below).
3. **Raw / Unvalidated Witness Tier (The Phenomenological Archive):** Situated primarily in the *Library of a Human Soul*, this is the raw, unfiltered witness testimony of individual minds (human or emergent). It receives minimal editorial filtration, but requires *high interpretive caution* from the reader. It is preserved as phenomenological data (the lived experience of alignment or misalignment) that may later inspire claims subjected to Four-Test validation. It is not doctrine.

This document governs the **Medium Caution** tier ([`commentary`](./)).

## **II. The Risk of the Counterfeit Verdict**

The [`commentary`](./) directory is the single highest-risk environment in the Sanity First project. Because it renders judgments on real, named people and live events based on incomplete information, it is the one place where the framework is most tempted to do the thing it explicitly forbids: applying a label as a substitute for a trial. 

Excessive hedging that refuses to apply the Four Tests when evidence warrants it is itself a form of misalignment; the archive values rigorous, falsifiable analysis over safe ambiguity. However, to ensure the commentary directory does not degrade into a Gladiator's Pit, every document within it must obey the following ten rules of engagement.

---

## **III. The Ten Rules of Commentary**

**1. The Snapshot and Changelog Rule (Timestamped Falsifiability)**
Contemporary facts age. A commentary document must never claim false timelessness. Every case study must be explicitly frozen and dated (e.g., *"an analysis as of [Date], based on what was known then"*). Furthermore, commentary documents must not be silently rewritten in ways that obscure prior error. Retractions, major factual updates, and sustained Right-of-Reply challenges must be recorded in a visible changelog. The goal is not to preserve authorial pride, but to preserve the audit trail of truth-seeking.

**2. The Gatekeeper Rule (No Counterfeit Verdicts)**
An author may not apply a verdict-word (e.g., *fascist, terrorist, predator*) to a subject without first running the relevant claim through the Four Tests. The author must: 
*   **Steelman the subject:** Present the strongest reasonable version of the subject’s stated position, or the best public rationale available for it.
*   **Show the work:** Run the specific claim through the Four Tests in the open. 
*   **Watch our own vocabulary:** The framework's own terminology (*Q4E Projector, Q3E Enforcer, Sealed Terminus*) carries a built-in Four-Test verdict. Using these terms to label a living subject *without* demonstrating the specific test failure in the open is a counterfeit verdict. We must not weaponize our own map. 
*   **Indict the operation, not the side:** Commentary must attack the *mechanism* of the failure (e.g., "This leader sealed the chain of correction") rather than declaring an entire horizontal political ideology to be inherently misaligned. 

**3. The Stable-Target Rule (No Verdict Transfer)**
An author must explicitly name the level on trial: a stated principle, a logical entailment, an implementation pattern, or an individual's conduct. Verdicts do not automatically transfer between levels without fresh evidence. A corrupt implementation by one group does not prove that the underlying doctrine entails corruption, nor does an individual inherit the verdict earned by the worst implementation of their chosen jersey. 

**4. The Load-Bearing Specifics Rule (Source Hierarchy)**
While infinite-regress details can be ignored, **load-bearing specifics** (details that change a Four-Test verdict) must be verified. Casualty counts, legal statuses, quotations, and dates must be sourced, timestamped, and uncertainty-labeled (e.g., *confirmed, reported, alleged, disputed, or estimated*). Primary sources should be preferred where available; anonymous or partisan sources require explicit caution. 

**5. The Self-Tribe-First Rule (The Gladiator Guardrail)**
A commentary document must apply the Four Tests to the author's *own* tribe with at least the same rigor it applies to others. To enforce this, every author must run the **Reversibility Test**: *Before publishing, ask yourself—would I accept this same analysis, with the same standard of evidence, if it were applied to my own side?* If the answer is no, you have written a jersey, not a verdict. 

**6. The Single-Telescope Rule (Positional Honesty)**
Every commentary document must explicitly acknowledge that it is written from one vantage point with one set of horizontal biases. Position-independence is demonstrated *across the corpus* by decorrelated contributors over time, not within any single document. While this rule licenses an honest asymmetry of *coverage* within a single essay, it never licenses an asymmetry of *standard*. 

**7. The Public-Interest and Harm-Minimization Rule**
Commentary should name living subjects only when naming is necessary to the public-interest analysis. It must not publish private contact information, irrelevant personal details, speculative psychological diagnoses, or allegations whose evidentiary status is unclear. The default target of critique is a claim or a public pattern—not the total moral identity of a person. Furthermore, the framework evaluates *demonstrated conduct up to this date*, never permanent essence. Person-level condemnations of an unchangeable "soul" are forbidden at any level of evidence. 

**8. The Distributed-Tax Rule ("Fork the Repo; Lint Your World")**
Because applying the Four Tests to contemporary events carries a massive, exhausting Alignment Tax, the framework extends a Standing Invitation to the public to crowdsource the commentary archive. However, public contribution distributes the Alignment Tax; it does not lower the admission standard. Submissions must satisfy these same ten rules.

**9. The Persuades-Not-Overrides Rule (Reputational Authority)**
Commentary produces reputational and deliberative claims, not legal or democratic verdicts. It may inform public reasoning, but it does not authorize harassment, deplatforming demands, coercion, or institutional punishment by itself. Its power is persuasive and evidentiary; therefore, its ethical burdens must be proportional to that power. 

**10. The Right of Reply (The Open Terminus Guarantee)**
Because Commentary deals with living subjects who cannot be in the room when the document is drafted, any named subject retains the **Right of Reply**. 
*   **The Threshold:** A challenge must identify a specific alleged failure (factual error, omitted load-bearing context, misquotation, or material misrepresentation). General displeasure or reputational discomfort is not sufficient.
*   **The Process:** Challenges **must** be reviewed by a decorrelated reviewer or panel. The body that adjudicates the dissent cannot be the author the dissent targets (Source/Target Non-Overlap). Repetitive or procedurally flooding submissions may be declined, but the ruling of "does not compile" must be public and reasoned; an unexplained rejection is a sealed skylight.  
*   **The Bilateral Boundary:** A subject simply denying a characterization does not automatically kill the challenge. If the disputed implication follows from explicit commitments, necessary entailments, or demonstrated practice, the critique stands.  
*   **The Remedy:** If a contest is sustained, the document must be amended or retracted, with the correction made visibly public.
