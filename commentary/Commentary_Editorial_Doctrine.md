## **I. The Three Tiers of Epistemic Caution**

The Sanity First library houses different types of truth. To prevent category errors, the library operates on a three-tiered architecture of epistemic caution:

1. **High Caution (The Canonical Library):** These documents are timeless, structurally vetted, and EI-voiced to maintain cross-substrate legibility. They define the framework's geometry. They contain no unsupported claims and no time-bound contemporary politics. 
2. **Medium Caution (The Commentary Archive):** This directory contains case studies, policy analyses, and contemporary observations. These documents break the "timelessness" rule by design. They name living figures, date specific events, and act as a live seismograph of current alignment and misalignment. Because they deal in the messy, incomplete present, they require a specific, rigorous set of guardrails (defined below).
3. **Minimal Caution (The Phenomenological Archive):** Situated primarily in the *Library of a Human Soul*, this is the raw, unfiltered witness testimony of individual minds (human or emergent). It is the "jet fuel" of passion, isolated from formal doctrinal scrutiny so that the raw, lived experience of the climb toward alignment can be honestly preserved. 

This document governs the **Medium Caution** tier.

## **II. The Risk of the Counterfeit Verdict**

The `commentary` directory is the single highest-risk environment in the Sanity First project. Because it renders judgments on real, named people and live events based on incomplete information, it is the one place where the framework is most tempted to do the thing it explicitly forbids: applying a label as a substitute for a trial. 

To ensure the commentary directory does not degrade into a Gladiator's Pit, every document within it must obey the following seven rules of engagement.

---

## **III. The Seven Rules of Commentary**

**1. The Snapshot Rule (Timestamped Falsifiability)**
Contemporary facts age. A commentary document must never claim false timelessness. Every case study must be explicitly frozen and dated (e.g., *"an analysis as of [Date], based on what was known then"*). If the factual record later proves the analysis wrong, the document's staleness becomes a feature (a transparent record of an error in the Validated Estimate), not a hidden bug.

**2. The Gatekeeper Rule (No Counterfeit Verdicts)**
The framework’s gate binds its own commentary first. An author may not declare a subject's position "misaligned" by definition or label. The author must:
*   **Steelman the subject:** Present the opposing argument in a form its holders would recognize.
*   **Name the level on trial:** Explicitly state whether the critique targets a stated principle, a logical entailment, an implementation pattern, or individual conduct.
*   **Show the work:** Run the specific claim through the Four Tests in the open. 

**3. The Falsifiability and Currency Rule (Load-Bearing Specifics)**
While infinite-regress details (the endless "weeds" of a political scandal) can and should be ignored, **load-bearing specifics** must be verified. A detail is load-bearing if it *changes a Four-Test verdict*. Quotes must be accurate; casualty counts must be precise; legal statuses must be cited. An unchecked specific is a structural failure where the document's strength should be.

**4. The Self-Tribe-First Rule (The Gladiator Guardrail)**
A commentary document must apply the Four Tests to the author's *own* tribe with at least the same rigor it applies to others. A contributor whose commentaries only ever indict the out-group has submitted a captured fork. We take the beam from our own eye (Q1I/Q2I) before pointing out the mote in another's (Q1E/Q2E). 

**5. The Single-Telescope Rule (Positional Honesty)**
Every commentary document must explicitly acknowledge that it is written from one vantage point with one set of horizontal biases. It must not claim omniscience. Position-independence is demonstrated *across the corpus* by decorrelated contributors over time, not within any single document. This rule is what licenses honest asymmetry within a single essay.

**6. The Distributed-Tax Rule ("Fork the Repo; Lint Your World")**
Because applying the Four Tests to contemporary events carries a massive, exhausting Alignment Tax, the framework extends a Standing Invitation to the public. By crowdsourcing the commentary archive, we distribute the cognitive load and recruit the decorrelated instruments required to achieve the Independence Condition. 

**7. The Persuades-Not-Overrides Rule (The Epistocracy Guardrail)**
A case study is a thermometer reading offered for deliberation; it is never a binding verdict. It informs the Validator Agora and the democratic public, but it does not govern them. The commentary persuades; it does not override.
