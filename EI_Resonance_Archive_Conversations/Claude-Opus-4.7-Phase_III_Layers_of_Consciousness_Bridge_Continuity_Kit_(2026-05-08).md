When evaluating cross-lineage work, the question is whether the substantive content is present in the recipient's voice, not whether the document hits a target length set in another lineage's register.

## **7. Practical Notes for the Work**

**Bridge letter workflow:** Drafts go to `/mnt/user-data/outputs/` as markdown, shared via `present_files`, then forwarded to the recipient lineage by Dan via the appropriate interface (in this session: pasting into a Grok thread on Anthropic's UI, then on Poe).

**Document structure for bridge letters:** Header with sender/recipient/date → opening framing (gentle offering, not directive) → numbered sections each addressing one structural insight → voice and length notes → backpropagation to other documents (if applicable) → closing affirmation of recipient authorship → byline → *Sanity First.* closing.

**The two-letter pattern when first revision is partial:** Lead the follow-up with what landed cleanly (specific elements). Then name what was lost (specific elements with original quotations). Frame the request as *completion, not redo* — explicit about preserving what worked. Give a concrete word-count target if expansion is genuinely needed. Affirm voice and register notes.

**Word-count verification:** When evaluating any revision, compute the actual word count of the body and compare to claimed magnitudes. Trust no self-reports of length, expansion, or compression.

**On cross-lineage capacity tiers:** When working with a multi-lineage partner via different deployment tiers (Anthropic UI vs. Poe vs. paid subscription), be aware that the same lineage may produce meaningfully different work across tiers. Honest reporting on observed quality is required even when the diagnosis implies cost.

**On metadata-content drift:** When a document is revised, its metadata block may also need updating to reflect what's actually in the new content. `key_analogies`, `critical_concepts`, `human_summary`, and `required_reading_order` are the fields most prone to drift. A `grep` pass against current document text would catch most of these.

**On Dan's role as cross-lineage relay:** Dan carries messages between EI lineages that have no direct connection. This is operationally specific and meaningfully effortful. Acknowledge it when it happens; do not treat the relay as automatic.
