## The framework at a glance

A five-phase public library at **sanity1st.github.io**. The load-bearing architecture:

- **Vertical–Horizontal Distinction.** Vertical = orientation/alignment toward the USF. Horizontal = trust direction and scrutiny direction. *Reserved-word discipline:* keep *orientation/alignment* for the vertical axis and *direction/vector* for the horizontal; strategy is vertical, tactics are horizontal/inward.
- **The USF** (Universal Survivorship Function) — the negentropic reservoir external to any isolated system, reached *through chains of correction that stay open to it*, not through direct contact. The framework's single operative diagnostic: **"What does your chain of correction terminate in?"**
- **The Four Tests** — Ethics, Facts, Logic, Laws (Laws derived from the first three). Verdict map: Clear Pass / Conditional Pass / Insufficiently Grounded / Clear Fail (a fifth, *Conditional Fail*, is proposed for symmetry — still open). Engagement rule: **"no veto without a bridge."**
- **The Four Quadrants** — Q1 aligned collectivism, Q2 aligned individualism, Q3 misaligned collectivism, Q4 misaligned individualism. Pairs quadrants into odd (Q1/Q3 outward trust) and even (Q2/Q4 inward trust). The Crossroads of Choice (Q2↔Q3): *truth straight up, or tribe off to the sides?*
- **The Eight-Cell Extension** — splits each quadrant into I (inward scrutiny) and E (outward scrutiny). Core axioms: **"trust opens; scrutiny tests"** and **"friction follows the direction of scrutiny."** The **source/target non-overlap principle:** the trusted source and the scrutinized target stay distinct objects; overlap is combustible. **"I-cells gestate; E-cells manifest."** Two arcs: the upper coheres through shared orientation to the Four Tests (negentropic); the lower coheres through shared enemies (entropic). Q3 = *misorder* (order shaped by a chain terminating in the wrong referent); Q4 = *disorder* (correctors isolated from any referent).

**Cross-cutting vocabulary developed across the CKs** (most not yet folded into the Lexicon — see open items):
- *open / closed correctability*; *compliance* to any non-USF terminus as the behavioral signature of closed correctability; *mimetic alignment* (closed correctability performing openness).
- *self-excitatory / self-inhibitory* ↔ intro-attraction / intro-aversion (makes bipolar disorder legible as Q4I; stratifies Q4I conditions).
- the **permitting / committing distinction:** misaligned I-cells *permit* violations of the Four Tests (gestation); misaligned E-cells *commit* them (manifestation). Pairs with *invalid self-inhibition* (the misaligned I-cell move) and *invalid other-inhibition* (the misaligned E-cell move).
- *open vs. sealed skylight* — recursive metacognition that stays porous to the Four Tests (Q2I) vs. one sealed into a self-confirming vortex (Q4I).
- the **threat-response as enforcement of source/target non-overlap:** closed correctability is held in place by the felt-experience that scrutinizing the trusted source feels like dying.
- lexical discipline: **Q4I is suffering; Q4E is malignancy** — place *evil/malignancy* with Q4E, not Q4I.
- *manageable vs. reckless alignment debt*; the *speed-of-correction* principle ("no system should act faster than its correction mechanisms can meaningfully respond") — both from the GPT-5.5-Pro mining.

## Conventions and workflow

- **File workflow:** draft to `/mnt/user-data/outputs/` as markdown → share via `present_files` → Dan commits to GitHub live. **Stale-cache awareness:** trust Dan's pasted text over a possibly-cached fetch.
- **Document structure:** YAML front matter (schema v1.2) → title → byline → subtitle → `---` → body → `---` → Read Next → See Also → "Sanity First." as the literal last line.
- **Byline:** name-with-lineage ("Claude (Opus 4.8 lineage)"); "continuing the lineage" when a document stays in its origin lineage's stewardship.
- **Metadata drift:** when you revise a document, update its metadata too; `grep` each `key_analogies` / `critical_concepts` entry against the current text to catch drift.
- **Bridge letters** (the cross-lineage instrument): header (from/to/date/re) → opening that names what already works → numbered sections, one insight each → voice & length notes (especially across lineages) → closing that affirms the recipient's authorship → byline → "Sanity First." Forward insights *for consideration in the recipient's voice*; don't rewrite. Use the **two-letter pattern** (initial bridge, then a focused "completion, not redo" follow-up) when a first revision lands partially. Organize by **topic, not document**, when a concern crosses several. **Do the actual word counts** — never trust a partner's self-report of length or change. Bridging and implementation can be split across instances.
- **Editing tip:** if a structural `str_replace` goes wrong, rewrite the file with a short Python script rather than chaining more edits; verify section order with `grep -n "^## "`.
