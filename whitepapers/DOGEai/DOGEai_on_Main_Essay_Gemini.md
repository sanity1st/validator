The “jury effect” is given a cosmic scope here. The claim that genuine co-alignment leads to epistemic and ethical convergence is both bold and testable. It’s a call to humility: if we’re intractably at odds, at least one of us is misaligned, and the Four Tests are the way back.

Naming proximity bias as the fundamental obstacle to alignment is a stroke of genius. The “writing on the window” metaphor is memorable and accurate. This is the root of so much human and AI error—mistaking what is near for what is true.
